Troubleshooting

Before seeking assistance, review the following problems and solutions.

  • Also refer to the instructions for the game or accessory in question.
Q:

Can I configure the System Settings for Wii U from the Wii Menu as well?
A:

No. System Settings for Wii U are configured using the Wii U Menu.
Q:

Can I use the Wii U GamePad?
A:

It can be used to display the Wii Menu or as a screen when playing Wii software, but it cannot be used as a controller.
Q:

Can I use the Wii U Pro Controller?
A:

The Wii U Pro Controller cannot be used on the Wii Menu or while playing Wii software.
Q:

Why does the image quality change when I switch to the Wii Menu?
A:

The Wii Menu and Wii software are displayed in standard definition (not high definition) by design.
Q:

Can I turn the Wii U GamePad screen on and off?
A:

Press the POWER Button on the Wii U GamePad to turn the screen on or off while in the Wii Menu or while using Wii software.

A:

When the Wii Menu is displayed or Wii software is in use, press and hold the POWER Button on the Wii U console or the Wii Remote for at least one second to turn off the Wii U console. Press the POWER Button on the Wii U GamePad to turn the GamePad screen off or on.
Q:

Is it possible to use WiiConnect24?
A:

No.
Q:

Can the Photo Channel and Internet Channel be used?
A:

No.
Q:

Why can't I create, send, or receive messages in the Wii Message Board?
A:

You can only use the Wii Message Board to view messages posted to your calendar. You cannot create, send, or receive messages.
Q:

Can Nintendo GameCube™ Game Discs or controllers be used?
A:

No.
Q:

Can Mii characters that were created with Mii Maker on the Wii U console be used with Wii software?
A:

Mii characters that were created with Wii U can only be used with Wii U software.
Q:

Can Mii characters in the Mii Channel within the Wii Menu be used with Wii U software?
A:

Yes, as long as the software is designed with this functionality. To use your characters from the Mii Channel with Wii U software, you must copy them into the Mii Maker application on the Wii U Menu. For details, see the electronic manual for Mii Maker.
Q:

Why am I having trouble using the Nunchuk™ controller and Classic Controller™ (including the Classic Controller Pro)?
A: Please check the following:
  • Does the software you are playing support the Nunchuk or the Classic Controller? If you don't see a compatibility icon for these accessories on the packaging (for packaged software sold at retail) or the Wii Shop Channel screen (for digital downloads), you won't be able to use these accessories with the game in question.
  • Is the plug fully inserted? Make sure the plug on the Nunchuk or Classic Controller is inserted fully into the connector on the Wii Remote. Make sure you press until you hear a click.

If you have checked the issues above and the accessory still doesn't function normally, there is a chance that the "neutral position" for your controller is out of alignment. To reset the neutral position, press , , , and simultaneously for three seconds during gameplay, making sure not to touch the control stick or the other buttons during this time.

  • The term "neutral position" refers to the state when the control stick is centered and no buttons are being pressed, as it is when you hold the controller while not touching the control stick or pressing buttons. If you are tilting the control stick or pressing buttons when the console is turned on, the console considers that state to be the neutral position, which causes unexpected results during gameplay.
Q:

Which types of SD Cards can be used with the Wii U console?
A:

The Wii U console supports the following types of SD Cards:

  • SD Memory Cards (up to 2 GB)
  • SDHC Memory Cards (up to 32 GB)
Q:

Why isn't my SD Card being recognized? Why isn't data on the card being displayed?
A: Try removing and reinserting the SD Card on which your Wii software's data is saved, making sure it is inserted correctly. If the software or data on your SD Card still isn't being recognized, try using it with another device that supports that SD Card (for example, a computer or digital camera).

If the other device also has problems accessing the card, the SD Card might be corrupted. If the other device can access the card, the SD Card slot on your Wii U console may be damaged or the data saved on the SD Card may be corrupt. In this case, your console may need to be repaired. Please visit support.nintendo.com for troubleshooting and repair options.

Note that the following Wii software titles are not compatible with SDHC Memory Cards. For these titles, you will need to use an SD Memory Card instead.
  • Excite Truck™
  • Endless Ocean™
  • Super Smash Bros.™ Brawl
  • Guitar Hero®: World Tour
  • Animal Crossing™: City Folk
  • Rock Band® 2
Q:

Is it possible to increase the amount of free space in Wii system memory or on an SD Card?
A:

Yes, you can erase unnecessary data from the Wii Options screen. However, please note that save data for certain games cannot be copied to SD Cards.
Q:

Why can't I purchase software from the Wii Shop Channel?
A: If you don't have sufficient Wii Points™ in your account to download specific software, you will need to add more Wii Points through one of the methods listed below.
  • Purchase a Wii Points Card™ from a game store or electronics retailer, and then register the activation number on the back of that card in the Wii Shop Channel.
  • Purchase Wii Points directly from the Wii Shop Channel.
  • Wii Points are not added to your account automatically when you purchase a Wii Points Card. Visit support.nintendo.com for details about how to redeem a Wii Points Card.
  • To purchase Wii Points directly, a credit card (VISA or MasterCard) is required. If you have restricted Wii Shopping Services setting (under Wii Menu Settings in Parental Controls), follow the on-screen instructions to temporarily deactivate the restriction.
Q:

Can Wii Points that have been added using the Wii Shop Channel also be used with Nintendo eShop?
A: No. Wii Points can only be used with the Wii Shop Channel. To purchase software through Nintendo eShop, add funds to your Nintendo eShop balance using one of the methods below:

  • Nintendo Prepaid Card
  • Credit Card
