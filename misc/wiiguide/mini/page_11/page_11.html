Game Ratings

PEGI Age Rating System

The Pan European Game Information (PEGI) icons indicate age ratings and content that may be inappropriate for users below certain ages. Please check for these icons when purchasing software.

You can restrict the software that can be used on the Wii mini console based on your children's ages (→).

PEGI AGE RATINGS
Titles rated 3 are suitable for ages 3 and older
Titles rated 7 are suitable for ages 7 and older
Titles rated 12 are suitable for ages 12 and older
Titles rated 16 are suitable for ages 16 and older

Titles rated 18 are suitable for ages 18 and older
  • The PEGI rating does not indicate the difficulty of a game; it provides information about the age suitability of a game based on its content.
  • The PEGI system was established to help parents and guardians make informed decisions when buying computer and video games. It replaced a number of national age rating systems with a single system now used throughout most of Europe. For further information about PEGI, visit www.pegi.info.

Age Rating System in Germany:

"Unterhaltungssoftware Selbstkontrolle" (USK)

Computer Games in Germany are checked and rated by the USK according to §14 JuSchG (German Youth Protection Act). According to statutory provisions, the corresponding age rating icon must always be shown on the packaging and, usually, the storage media. For Wii games, the USK icon is shown on the disc. On Nintendo DS, Nintendo DSi™ and Nintendo 3DS Game Cards, the ratings "Freigegeben ohne Altersbeschränkung" (no age restriction) and "Freigegeben ab 6 Jahren" (not recommended for people younger than 6 years of age) do not need to be displayed; higher USK ratings are displayed in a shortened written form on the Game Card.
